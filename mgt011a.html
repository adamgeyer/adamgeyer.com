Core equation
Accounting Equation
Assets = Liabilities + Equity
Foundation of all double-entry accounting. Every transaction keeps this balanced.
Retained Earnings
Beg RE + Revenue − Expenses − Dividends = End RE
Connects income statement to balance sheet via stockholders' equity.
Income statement
Net Revenue
Total Revenue − Returns − Allowances − Discounts
Returns, allowances, and discounts are contra revenue accounts (debit normal balance).
Gross Profit
Net Revenue − Cost of Goods Sold
Operating Income
Gross Profit − Operating Expenses
Excludes non-operating items like interest income/expense.
Net Income
All Revenues − All Expenses
Bottom line. Flows into Retained Earnings.
Receivables — Ch.5
Net Accounts Receivable
AR − Allowance for Uncollectible Accounts
AFUA is a contra asset (credit normal balance). NOT a liability.
Interest on Notes
Face Value × Annual Rate × (Days / 365)
Use the exact fraction of the year the note is outstanding.
Receivables Turnover
Net Credit Sales ÷ Average AR
Higher = faster cash collection from customers.
Average Collection Period
365 ÷ Receivables Turnover
Days the average AR balance is outstanding.
Inventory — Ch.6
COGS Formula
Beg Inventory + Purchases − Ending Inventory
Under FIFO, beginning inventory is sold first. Don't forget to include it.
Inventory Turnover
COGS ÷ Average Inventory
Average = (Beg + End) ÷ 2. Use average, not ending balance.
Average Days in Inventory
365 ÷ Inventory Turnover
Gross Profit Ratio
Gross Profit ÷ Net Sales
Higher ratio = more profitable inventory management.
Long-term assets — Ch.7
Straight-Line Depreciation
(Cost − Residual Value) ÷ Useful Life
Land is NEVER depreciated. Depreciation = cost allocation, not value decrease.
Book Value
Cost − Accumulated Depreciation
Gain / Loss on Disposal
Proceeds − Book Value at Disposal Date
Positive = gain. Negative = loss. Always update depreciation before disposal.
Return on Assets
Net Income ÷ Average Total Assets
Profit Margin
Net Income ÷ Net Sales
Asset Turnover
Net Sales ÷ Average Total Assets
Liabilities — Ch.8–9
Interest Expense
Face Value × Annual Rate × (Months / 12)
Count the first month. September to December = 4 months, not 3.
Working Capital
Current Assets − Current Liabilities
Current Ratio
Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities
Measures ability to pay current obligations.
Acid-Test Ratio
(Cash + Investments + AR) ÷ Current Liabilities
Excludes inventory and prepaid. More conservative liquidity measure.
Debt to Equity Ratio
Total Liabilities ÷ Stockholders' Equity
Higher = more financial risk/leverage.
Ch.5 · AFUA
✗ "AFUA has a credit balance, so it must be a liability."
✓ AFUA is a CONTRA ASSET. It reduces Accounts Receivable on the balance sheet. Both liabilities and contra assets have credit normal balances — don't confuse them.
Ch.5 · Write-off
✗ Recording Bad Debt Expense when writing off a specific account.
✓ Bad debt was already expensed when the allowance was estimated at year-end. Write-off only removes AR and reduces AFUA — no income statement effect.
Ch.6 · FIFO
✗ Forgetting beginning inventory when calculating FIFO COGS.
✓ Beginning inventory was purchased BEFORE any current-period buys — under FIFO it is always sold first. Include it as your first batch.
Ch.6 · Inventory Turnover
✗ Using ending inventory instead of average inventory.
✓ Use average inventory: (Beginning + Ending) ÷ 2. COGS is a full-period amount; the balance sheet item must be averaged over the same period.
Ch.7 · Depreciation concept
✗ "Depreciation records the decrease in market value of an asset."
✓ Depreciation is COST ALLOCATION, not valuation. We allocate the asset's cost to expense over its useful life — market value is irrelevant.
Ch.7 · Land
✗ Depreciating land along with buildings and equipment.
✓ Land is NEVER depreciated — it has an indefinite useful life. Land improvements (parking lots, fences) ARE depreciated separately.
Ch.7 · Asset disposal
✗ Forgetting to update depreciation before recording the asset sale.
✓ Always record depreciation up to the exact disposal date first. Otherwise book value is overstated and your gain/loss will be wrong.
Ch.3 · Depreciation timing
✗ "March 1 to December 31 is 9 months (12 − 3 = 9)."
✓ March 1 to December 31 is 10 months — count every month from March through December. The shortcut of subtracting month numbers loses the first month.
Ch.8 · Interest months
✗ "September to December = 3 months (12 − 9 = 3)."
✓ Count September, October, November, December = 4 months. Always count the starting month.
Ch.8 · Deferred Revenue
✗ "Deferred Revenue is a revenue account because it has the word 'revenue' in it."
✓ Deferred Revenue is a LIABILITY. Cash was received but goods/services haven't been provided yet — the company owes the customer.
Ch.4 · NSF Check
✗ Thinking the NSF check is a bad check written BY the company.
✓ NSF means a customer's check to you bounced. Reverse your cash deposit: Debit AR / Credit Cash. The customer still owes you the money.
Ch.4 · Bank Reconciliation
✗ Adjusting the company's cash balance for deposits outstanding and checks outstanding.
✓ Outstanding deposits and checks go on the BANK side — the company already has these recorded. Only adjust the company side for items the bank has recorded that the company hasn't yet.
